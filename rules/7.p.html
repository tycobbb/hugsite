Impermanence

Hopefully that was okay. In this game, you mark, draw on, bend, burn, label, or otherwise change and destroy cards. You might feel precious about them. That’s okay. Good, even.

While you start with one deck, it won’t be the deck that you end up with. You will lose cards, but you’ll gain new ones, too. There may come a time when you want to save a card forever. You should. However, keep mind that you will want to have enough cards on hand to play with someone, should you be asked.

Your cards are as much a part of the game as are your tools for changing them. Choose tools you gravitate towards. A sharpie and a shard of danburite are nice. If a ballpoint pen is your thing, use that. Whatever works, as long as it can make a memorable mark on a card.
